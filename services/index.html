Instructions for Water Heater Expansion Tank Installation:
The expansion tank is usually installed directly above the water heater using a tee installed in the cold water inlet. The expansion tank is usually installed horizontally, although it can be installed vertically if required due to limited space. The plumbing fittings you'll need depend on the type of plumbing you have and the orientation of the expansion tank, but the most common connections are made with copper pipes and fittings. The expansion tank itself usually has a 3/4" threaded fitting that connects to the cold water pipe with a tee and short lengths or threaded or soldered pipes.

Attach dielectric coupling
Once the water heater is properly positioned, attach the dielectric sleeve to the cold water inlet. Wrap the threads of the fitting with sealing tape, then screw it into the cold water inlet on the water heater using a pipe wrench or pliers.

Connect the copper adapter
Wrap a few loops of pipe sealing tape around the top threads of the connection, then thread the female copper adapter onto the union. Tighten securely with a pipe wrench or pliers.

Attach tee
Install the short copper pipe and tee to the adapter on the dielectric fitting of the water heater. Typically, the tee should be about 30 cm above the water heater to provide enough clearance for the expansion tank. Most plumbers make these connections by soldering afterwards, but they can also be made with push-in connections (sometimes called "shark bite").

Attach the expansion tank pipe
Attach a short length of horizontal copper pipe to the side outlet of the tee using soldering or push-fit connections. The length of this copper pipe will depend on the space available, but it's best to keep it as short as possible - no more than 6 inches or so. At the end of the horizontal pipe, attach the female adapter using soldering or a tight joint.

Attach expansion tank
Wrap the pipe sealing tape around the expansion tank threaded fitting, then screw it onto the threaded adapter on the horizontal pipe. Usually hand-tightening is sufficient; be careful not to overtighten or damage pipes or fittings.

Complete the installation
Complete the installation by connecting the top outlet of the tee to the cold water supply using all necessary pipes and fittings. Many plumbers make this connection with copper flexible tubing rather than rigid tubing.
